ROBO RACER

Event Overview

The Bot Race Competition challenges teams to build and control a manually operated robot to navigate a racetrack filled with obstacles. The goal is to complete the track in the shortest possible time while avoiding penalties. The competition tests precision, speed, and adaptability in maneuvering through ramps, bridges, and other challenging terrains.

Rules and Regulations
  1. Team Composition:
    • Each team can have a maximum of 4 members.
    • Only 2 members are allowed near the bot during the race.
    • One team member must be elected as the robot handler‚Äîonly this member is allowed to control the bot during the game.
    • All other team members must remain outside the game zone.

  2. Robot Specifications:
    • The maximum bot dimensions must not exceed 300mm x 300mm x 300mm (Length x Breadth x Height).
    • Bots exceeding this limit will be disqualified.
    • The bot must be manually controlled and can be either wired or wireless.
    • The bot must not be made from Lego parts or any ready-made assembly kits.
    • The machine must be electrically powered‚Äîuse of IC engines is not allowed.
    • The power source can be either fixed on the robot or connected via a cord to a stationary power source.
    • The potential between any two points on the robot must not exceed 24V.

  3. Gameplay Rules:
    • The robot starts from the start line as soon as the timer begins.
    • The robot must stay on track; if it goes off, it must restart from the last checkpoint crossed.
    • Skipping obstacles incurs penalties that will be added to the final time.
    • The timer stops once the robot crosses the finish line.
    • Participants must carry spare batteries to avoid disqualification if the bot is not ready or stops mid-race.
    • For wired bots, the wire must be long enough and remain slack throughout the race.

  4. Race Track & Obstacles:
    • The track has one Start and one Finish line.
    • The surface may have unevenness and obstacles designed to slow down the robot.
    • Possible obstacles include Steep ramps, Bridges ,Speed breakers, Marble pits, Slippery paths, Rotating discs, Curve ramp downs, Seesaws

  5. Conduct & Fair Play:
    • Participants must behave professionally; misbehavior may lead to disqualification.
    • No deliberate interference with other robots or damage to the arena is allowed.
    • Decisions by the Organizing Committee are final and must be respected by all teams.

Judging Criteria

  • The winner is determined based on the fastest completion time after considering penalties.
  • Penalties are added for each skipped obstacle.
  • The robot must follow the track; if it goes off-course, it must restart from the last checkpoint crossed.
  • Timing stops when the robot crosses the finish line.
  • The Organizing Committee‚Äôs decision is final regarding scoring, gameplay, timing, and penalties.
