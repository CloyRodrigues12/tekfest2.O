ROBO SUMO

Event Overview

The Robo Sumo Competition challenges teams to design and build robots capable of pushing their opponent‚Äôs robot out of the designated play area. The event tests engineering skills, strategic planning, and real-time adaptability in a competitive yet fair environment. The team that accumulates the highest points through successful combat techniques wins the competition.

Rules and Regulations
  1. Team Composition:
    • Each team can have a maximum of 4 members.
    • Only two members are allowed near the arena during the match.

  2. Robot Specifications:
    • Maximum dimensions: 300mm x 300mm x 200mm (Length x Breadth x Height).
    • Maximum permissible weight: 5 kg (strictly no tolerance allowed).
    • Robots must remain intact throughout the match (no intentional splitting).
    • Negative clearance is not allowed.
    • The external controlling device is not included in the size constraints.
    • Robots can be wired or wireless.
    • Power sources can be:
      • Battery-operated (fixed on the robot) or
      • Stationary with a cord connecting the robot.
    • Maximum 3S LiPo battery allowed.
    • Participants must have spare batteries; failure to do so may lead to disqualification.
    • For wired robots:
      • The wire must be at least 3m long.
      • It must remain slack during the match to ensure smooth movement.

  3. Gameplay Rules:
    • Each match consists of two rounds, with each round lasting 2 minutes.
    • Two teams compete: Red Team and Blue Team.
    • A toss determines which side of the arena the winning team starts from.
    • Wire entanglement will pause the game, and the match will restart from the same bot positions.
    • Technical Breaks: Each team is allowed one technical break per match (including tie-breakers), lasting 120 seconds.
    • A 1-minute break is allowed between rounds.
    • Hand-touching the robot to prevent it from falling will result in an automatic fall, awarding points to the opponent.
    • The judge‚Äôs decision is final in case of disputes or ambiguities.

  4. Arena Setup:
    • The arena contains:
      • Two Bonus Zones (Yellow)
      • Green Circular Play Area (central metallic part)
    • Total Circular Play Area diameter: 1.5 meters.
    • Total arena size: 2m x 2m.

  5. Scoring System:
    • 1 point awarded if a robot falls outside the Green Circular Play Area.
    • 2 points awarded if a robot pushes an opponent out from the Bonus Zone (Yellow).
    • If both teams have equal points after two rounds, a tie-breaker round of 60 seconds will determine the winner with the highest points.

  6. Final Decision:
    • All decisions made by the judges and organizing committee are final and binding.
