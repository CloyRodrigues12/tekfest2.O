ROBO SOCCER

Event Overview

Robo Soccer is a thrilling bot-controlled football competition where teams design and operate robots to push a ball into the opponent‚Äôs goalpost while defending their own. The event tests a team's ability to control, maneuver, and strategize with their bot to outscore the opposing team.

Rules and Regulations

  1. Team Composition & Participation:
    • Each team can have one bot and a maximum of 4 members.
    • Only 2 members are allowed near the arena during the match.
    • Only one participant controls the bot throughout the match.

  2. Match Format:
    • The order of the matches will be on the basis of drawing lots at the venue (based on the entries).

  3. Conduct & Fair Play:
    • Misbehavior by any participant may result in disqualification.
    • Deliberate interference with other robots or arena damage leads to immediate disqualification.

  4. Robot Specifications:
    • The bot must fit within 30 cm x 30 cm x 30 cm (L x B x H).
    • The maximum weight of the bot is 5 kg.
    • The bot must be self-powered with 3S LiPo batteries.
    • Battery replacement is not allowed once the bot is placed on the field.
    • The bot must not be made from Lego parts or ready-made kits.
    • IC engines are not allowed‚Äîthe bot must be electrically powered.
    • Wedges as clamps are prohibited.
    • For wired bots, the wire must be long enough to cover the arena and remain slack during the match.
    • The minimum wire length is 2 meters.

  5. Clamp Specifications:
    • Minimum width at the base: about 15 cm.
    • Minimum depth: about 6 cm.
    • Clamp should be stationary (no moving parts).
    • The length should not exceed 10 cm.
    • The shape can vary, but the angle should not be lesser than 90¬∞.

  6. Gameplay Rules:
    • The bot must push the ball into the opponent‚Äôs goal while defending its own.
    • No carrying, lifting, or shielding the ball using any mechanism.
    • Ramming is only allowed when engaging with the ball.
    • Each match consists of two halves of 2 minutes each.
    • In case of a tie, an extra 30 seconds round is played‚Äîthe first team to score wins.
    • Each team gets one technical timeout of 1 minute 30 seconds, usable across both halves.

Judging Criteria

  • The team that scores the most goals within the match duration wins.
  • If the match ends in a tie, an extra round of 30 seconds is played, and the team that scores first wins.
  • Fair play is mandatory‚Äîdeliberate ramming (unless engaging with the ball) and shielding the ball will result in penalties.
  • The Organizing Committee‚Äôs decision is final regarding scoring, gameplay, and participation.
